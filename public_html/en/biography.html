Observing and Recording

Through my previous solo exhibitions I have tried to capture the existential energy released when a complete stranger turns into an object of my interest. This is also a question of object identification.

Crowds of people flowing like a body of water; in crowd, individuals’ energies are negated and their existences dissolved. I cannot remember how many people I have passed by, way away from remembering who they are. I was surrounded by others, but my attention grew stronger into myself.

Through this experience, I came to think that locality and culture are no longer distinguishable, and the face of the crowd a medium that contains the landscape of locality and culture of that age. Emotion, long-suppressed by the oppressive value system of logic, built up without any outlet, emotion is expressed in faces. I was first interested in the structural qualities of the human face, but later realized that this interest arose from a primitive force exerted within faces. Thus I searched for a way to express my findings.

This is why I chose painting despite its association with tradition. Passers-by are undoubtedly seen with eyes, but when that brief moment is over they can never be seen again. In order to capture brief existences born between image and spontaneity, I needed a direct and instinctive quality of painting at my fingertip.

Goethe, author of The Theory of Colours, who has influenced my understanding of color greatly, grouped colors into categories such as mineral, fish, insect, bird, mammal and human. He discovers that primary colors, frequently found on minerals, fish, insects and even birds no longer appear on mammal and humans. From this, I deduced that using primary colors could induce an instinctual response to my human audiences.

Painting is direct. My current way of working has been planned and designed to reach a deeper and more sincere level of understanding. When my purely observational sight finds a single face in a crowd, when I focus on that face I am able to capture its existential moment.

I now wish to depart from my previous focus on recognition, expand my emotion and focus more on the individual itself amongst the crowd. Nuclearized individuals of the contemporary world, the nuclei, the particles of the society, they (we) now choose to be alone. We avoid humane relations, our lifecycles change to make living alone more comfortable and convenient. Expressive potential without any outlet, nowhere to go, builds up within the individual. Our previous diseases were derived from an immune reaction, to protect ourselves from external threats such as viruses, which has now shifted to symptoms within the self, namely depression. The age of excluding others is over, but now the self exhausts the self, and violence is returned into the self. My search is to find and capture shapes of such symptoms of today.

Artist Note, Jaeyeol Han 2013
